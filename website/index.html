Form Meets Function

Stance Culture

Stance culture focuses on modifying vehicles for a lowered, wide appearance that emphasises style and individuality. Enthusiasts adjusts suspension, camber angles and fitment to create aesthetically pleasing builds.

Types of Fitments

Stance Fitment

Stance fitment refers to the negative camber of the car's suspension to tilt the tops of the wheels inward to create a more aggressive and unique look. This modification enhances the car's stance making it look lower, but it often sacrifices tire contact with the road during cornering which potentially affects performance and tire wear

Flush Fitment

Flush fitment occurs when the wheels and tires align perfectly with the bodywork, neither sticking out nor tucked in. This is one of the hardest fitments to achieve because it requires precise calculations for suspension travel and careful selection of wheel widths and offsets. It often involves custom modifications to the body.

Tuck Fitment

Tuck fitment features wheels that are completely tucked within the car's arches meaning the wheels and tires do not extend beyond the bodywork. Achieving this look may require modifications like rolling the arches or cutting fender liners especially when the car is lowered significantly.

Poke Fitment

Poke fitment is when the wheels and tires extend out beyond the bodywork. This look is often seen on cars before wide-body kits are installed, where the wheels visibly poke out. Once the wide arches are added the wheels are enclosed within the bodywork eliminating the poke.

Safety and Legal Compliance

Reduced Visibility

Reduced Visibility

Lowered ride height can obstruct the driver's view of the road, increasing the risk of accidents.

Increased Tyre Wear

Increased Tyre Wear

Extreme camber can cause uneven tire wear, which may lead to blowouts and reduced vehicle safety.

Compromised Suspension

Compromised Suspension

Modifications can weaken suspension systems, leading to instability and potential loss of control during driving.

Legal Compliance Issues

Legal Compliance Issues

Many states have strict regulations on vehicle height and modifications, risking fines or impoundment for non-compliance.

Insurance Challenges

Insurance Challenges

Modified cars may face higher premiums or denied claims if the modifications are deemed unsafe or illegal.

Road Damage

Road Damage

Excessively low cars can scrape on public roads and speed bumps, leading to potential damage to both the vehicle and infrastructure.

About

Stance culture is about expressing individuality through cars and encouraging people to break away from the norm and showcase their unique style. It originated from lowrider and Japanese car scenes and focuses on making cars look cool, often by lowering them and changing the wheels. While it started with performance improvements, it has evolved to prioritise how a car looks over how it drives. This culture celebrates creativity and personal expression which essentially turns cars into reflections of the owner's personality.
